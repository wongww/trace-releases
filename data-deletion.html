Trace — Data Deletion

Last updated: 13 July 2026

This page explains how to delete the data collected by Trace, a personal movement-mapping app. Trace records your location to build a private map of where you've been. Trace does not collect your name or email; each install is identified only by an anonymous ID.

How to request deletion

  1. In the app (fastest, deletes everything): open Trace → Settings → Delete my data → confirm. This permanently erases your on-device history, your cloud backup, and your anonymous account.
  2. Uninstall the app: removing Trace deletes all data stored on your device.
  3. By email: to delete data that was backed up to the cloud, email williamreceipt@gmail.com with the subject "Trace data deletion request." Your request will be completed within 30 days.

What gets deleted

  • Your entire recorded location history (GPS points).
  • Derived data: cities visited, street-exploration coverage, and statistics.
  • Any cloud backup stored for your install (in the app's Firebase storage, under your anonymous ID).
  • Your anonymous account identifier.

What is kept, and retention

  • Trace does not create user profiles and does not store names, emails, or advertising IDs, so there is no personal account data beyond the anonymous ID described above.
  • After in-app deletion, or once an emailed request is completed, no data is retained.
  • If you only uninstall the app, on-device data is removed immediately, but any cloud backup you previously enabled remains until you delete it in the app or request deletion by email.
  • Deletion requests are not retained beyond the time needed to process them (up to 30 days for email requests).
