Trace — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026

Trace ("the app") is a personal movement-mapping app that records where you go and helps you see how much of the places you've visited you've explored. This policy explains what data the app handles, how, and your choices. Trace is operated by an independent developer ("we", "us").

Summary

  • Trace records your device's location to build your personal map of where you've been.
  • Your data is stored on your device by default. It only leaves your phone if you enable cloud backup, or when the app looks up place/street information.
  • We do not sell your data, use it for advertising, or share it with third parties for their own purposes.
  • You can export or delete your data at any time.

Data we collect

  • Precise location (GPS), including in the background while the app is not open, so it can record your routes. Collection only happens while you have tracking turned on.
  • Physical activity / motion (e.g. walking, driving), used solely to detect when you are moving so the app can save battery and tag each route with its travel mode.
  • Derived information computed from your location, such as distance travelled and which cities/areas you have visited.

Trace does not collect your name, contacts, photos, or advertising identifiers, and does not include third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.

How your data is used

  • To display your recorded paths on a map and compute statistics (distance, days, per-mode totals).
  • To estimate how much of a city's walkable streets you have explored.
  • To optionally back up and restore your data if you enable cloud sync.

Where your data is stored and sent

  • On your device: all recorded points are kept in a local database on your phone. This is the primary and default storage.
  • Cloud backup (optional): if you enable sync, your recorded points are uploaded over the network to Google Firebase (Cloud Storage), associated with an anonymous, randomly generated account identifier — not your name or email. This backup is used only to store and restore your own data.
  • Map & place lookups: to show maps and calculate exploration, the app sends location coordinates to the map/data providers you use — which may include Google Maps, Mapbox, and OpenStreetMap services (Nominatim and Overpass) — so they can return map tiles, place names, and street data. These providers process the requests under their own privacy policies.

Third-party services

  • Google Firebase (Authentication & Cloud Storage) — firebase.google.com/support/privacy
  • Google Maps Platform — policies.google.com/privacy
  • Mapbox — mapbox.com/legal/privacy
  • OpenStreetMap Foundation — wiki.osmfoundation.org/wiki/Privacy_Policy

Data retention

Your location history remains on your device until you delete the app's data or uninstall it. If cloud backup is enabled, backed-up data remains in cloud storage until you delete it. You can remove local data by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling.

Your choices and controls

  • Tracking is off until you turn it on, and you can turn it off at any time.
  • You control location permissions (including "all the time") in your device settings.
  • You can export your data (GPX / GeoJSON) and keep or delete your own copies.
  • You can disable cloud sync to keep all data on-device.

Security

Cloud-stored data is protected so that it is only accessible to your own anonymous account. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect your information.

Children

Trace is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
